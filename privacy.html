Privacy Policy & POPIA Notice

Effective date: 4 May 2026  ·  Last updated: 4 May 2026

Fervent Digital Marketing ("Fervent", "we", "us", "our") respects your privacy and is committed to protecting the personal information you share with us. This notice sets out how we collect, use, store, share and protect personal information, and the rights you have in relation to that information under the Protection of Personal Information Act, 2013 ("POPIA") and other applicable laws.

1. Who we are

Fervent Digital Marketing is an independent creative studio founded in 2018 and operated by Minette Hartman, based in Hartbeespoort, North West, South Africa. For the purposes of POPIA, Fervent Digital Marketing is the Responsible Party in respect of personal information processed through this website and our client engagements.

2. Information Officer

Minette Hartman serves as the designated Information Officer for Fervent Digital Marketing. Any privacy-related queries, requests or complaints can be directed to her at minette@ferventdigital.co.za.

3. Personal information we collect

We may collect and process the following categories of personal information:

  • Contact details you submit voluntarily — name, email address, telephone or WhatsApp number, business name, project description — when you email us, message us on WhatsApp, or otherwise contact us through this site.
  • Correspondence — the content of emails, WhatsApp messages, briefs, files and any other materials you choose to share with us.
  • Technical information automatically collected when you visit our website — IP address, browser type and version, device type, operating system, referring URL, pages visited, date and time of access. This information is collected and processed by our hosting and security provider, Cloudflare, Inc., for the purposes of delivering the site, preventing abuse and analysing aggregate usage.
  • Marketing preferences — should you ever opt in to receive updates from us.

We do not knowingly collect special personal information (such as health, religious beliefs, biometric data) or personal information of children under the age of 18 through this website.

4. How we collect personal information

We collect personal information directly from you when you contact us, engage our services, or interact with our website. We do not purchase mailing lists or contact details from third parties. Limited technical information is collected automatically by our website infrastructure (Cloudflare) for security and operational purposes.

5. Lawful basis for processing

We process personal information on one or more of the following lawful bases recognised under POPIA:

  • Consent — where you have given clear, informed consent (for example, by emailing us a project enquiry or by opting in to marketing).
  • Necessary for a contract — to negotiate, conclude or perform a contract for our creative services.
  • Legitimate interests — for the proper conduct of our business, including responding to enquiries, maintaining records, securing our infrastructure, and protecting our intellectual property, provided your rights and interests are not overridden.
  • Legal obligation — to comply with applicable laws (for example, tax, anti-money-laundering or accounting record-keeping requirements).

6. How we use personal information

We use personal information to:

  • respond to your enquiry and discuss potential work;
  • provide our creative services and deliver projects;
  • issue quotes, invoices and manage payments;
  • maintain client and supplier records;
  • operate, secure and improve our website;
  • comply with legal, regulatory and accounting obligations; and
  • where permitted, send you relevant updates about our work (you can unsubscribe at any time).

7. Sharing with third parties

We do not sell your personal information. We may share personal information with carefully selected service providers ("Operators" under POPIA) who process information on our behalf, including:

  • Cloudflare, Inc. — hosting, content delivery, DNS, email routing and security for this website (privacy policy).
  • Google LLC (Gmail) — receipt and storage of incoming email correspondence.
  • WhatsApp / Meta Platforms, Inc. — messaging service when you choose to contact us via WhatsApp.
  • Accounting, banking and tax service providers — where engagement of our services results in invoicing.
  • Legal, professional or regulatory authorities — where required by law or to protect our rights.

We require all Operators to maintain appropriate technical and organisational security measures and to process personal information only as instructed by us.

8. Cross-border (international) transfers

Some of our service providers (notably Cloudflare, Google and Meta) operate globally and may process personal information outside South Africa, including in the United States and the European Union. Where this occurs, we rely on the lawful transfer mechanisms permitted under section 72 of POPIA, including binding corporate rules, standard contractual clauses, or your explicit consent where applicable. The countries to which information may be transferred have data-protection laws which may differ from those of South Africa.

9. Cookies & similar technologies

This website does not set advertising or tracking cookies. Our hosting provider, Cloudflare, may set strictly necessary cookies (for example, __cf_bm) to detect and mitigate automated abuse and to keep the site secure. These cookies are essential to the functioning and security of the site and do not require your consent under POPIA. They do not identify you personally and are not used for advertising. You can configure your browser to refuse all cookies, but parts of the site may not function correctly as a result.

10. How long we keep personal information

We retain personal information only for as long as is necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by applicable law. Typical retention periods:

  • Project enquiries that do not lead to engagement — up to 24 months, then securely deleted.
  • Active client records — for the duration of the engagement and for 5 years thereafter, in line with South African accounting and tax record-keeping obligations.
  • Invoicing and accounting records — minimum 5 years as required by the Tax Administration Act.
  • Website server / security logs — typically up to 30 days, retained by Cloudflare for security purposes.

11. Your rights under POPIA

As a data subject, you have the following rights:

  • To be notified that personal information about you is being collected, or that there has been an unauthorised access to your information.
  • Of access — to request confirmation of, and a description of, the personal information we hold about you.
  • To correction or deletion — to request correction of inaccurate, irrelevant, excessive or outdated information, or deletion / destruction of information we no longer have lawful grounds to hold.
  • To object to the processing of your personal information on reasonable grounds, or to direct marketing.
  • To withdraw consent previously given, at any time, without affecting the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal.
  • To data portability — where processing is automated and based on consent or contract, to receive your information in a structured, commonly used format.
  • To submit a complaint to the Information Regulator (see section 13).
  • To institute civil proceedings regarding alleged interference with the protection of your personal information.

12. Exercising your rights

To exercise any of the rights set out above, please contact our Information Officer at minette@ferventdigital.co.za. We may need to verify your identity before responding. We will respond to your request within a reasonable period and at the latest within the timeframes prescribed by POPIA.

Subject access requests under the Promotion of Access to Information Act, 2000 ("PAIA") may be directed to the same address. A PAIA Manual is available on request.

14. Security of personal information

We take reasonable, appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal information from loss, damage, unauthorised destruction, unlawful access or processing. These include encryption in transit (HTTPS/TLS), access controls, secure cloud infrastructure (Cloudflare), strong authentication and regular review of our security practices. No method of electronic transmission or storage is, however, completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

15. Updates to this notice

We may update this notice from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, legal requirements or otherwise. The "Last updated" date at the top will indicate when the notice was most recently revised. We encourage you to review this notice periodically.
